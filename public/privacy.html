Tide

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 July 2026

Tide is a fast, keyboard-first calendar client for Google Calendar, available at tide.naklitechie.com. This policy explains what data Tide accesses, where it goes, and what it does not do. Tide is deliberately minimal: it keeps as little of your data as possible, and most of your data never touches Tide's servers at all.

1. What Tide accesses

When you connect a Google account, Tide requests only the scopes it needs to be a calendar client:

  • Google Calendar (view and edit events — calendar.events, calendar.readonly): to show your calendars and events and to let you create or edit events.
  • Basic profile (openid, email): your email, name, and profile picture, used to label the connected account and to associate your Tide notes and preferences with you.

You can connect more than one Google account; each is requested with the same scopes.

2. Where your data lives

Your Google sign-in tokens stay in your browser. Sign-in uses Google's standard OAuth flow. The one-time exchange of the sign-in code for tokens is performed by Tide's server so that Google's application secret is never exposed to your browser — but the resulting access and refresh tokens are returned to your browser and stored only there (in your browser's local IndexedDB storage). Tide's server does not retain them.

Your calendar contents never touch Tide's servers. Calendar and event data is fetched directly between your browser and Google's API. Tide's servers do not receive, process, or store your events, attendees, or calendar contents.

Notes and preferences are stored by Tide. Notes you write inside Tide (the scratchpad, per-day notes, and private notes you add to events) and your display preferences (calendar colours, which calendars are shown) are stored in Cloudflare Workers KV, associated with your Google account identifier so that they are available to you across your devices. These are the only user data Tide stores on its servers. Notes on events you can edit in Google are also written into the Google event itself, at your action.

3. What Tide does not do

  • No advertising, and no selling or renting of your data.
  • No sharing of your data with third parties, except the infrastructure providers below that are strictly necessary to run the service.
  • No analytics or tracking of your browsing.
  • No use of your data to train AI or machine-learning models.

4. Google API Services — Limited Use

Tide's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Tide uses Google user data only to provide and improve the calendar features you see, and does not transfer or use it for advertising, credit, or any purpose beyond those user-facing features.

5. Infrastructure & sub-processors

  • Google LLC — the calendar data and sign-in you connect.
  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosts the Tide application and the storage used for your notes and preferences.

6. Data retention and deletion

  • Tokens & local data: using Tide's "Disconnect & erase all local data" action clears all connected accounts and tokens from your browser.
  • Notes: clearing a note in Tide deletes it from Tide's storage.
  • Full deletion: to delete all of your Tide-stored notes and preferences, contact us at the address below and we will remove them.
  • Revoking access: you can revoke Tide's access to your Google account at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

7. Security

All traffic is served over HTTPS. Sign-in tokens are kept in your browser rather than on Tide's servers, and stored notes and preferences are isolated per Google account so that one user cannot access another's data.

8. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date above will change. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.

9. Contact

Questions, or a request to delete your data: use our contact form, or email support@naklitechie.com.
